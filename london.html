LONDON

Londinium was established as a civilian town by the Romans about seven years after the Roman invasion of Britain. London, like Rome, was founded on the point of the river where it was narrow enough to bridge and the strategic location of the city provided easy access to much of Europe. In 410 AD the Roman occuption of Britain ended and the city was abandonded. In 1176 constrution began on London Bridge. The late 16th and early 17th century saw the great flourishing of drama in Londonw whose preeminent figure was Willam shakespeare. The Great Plague occured in 1665 and 1666, killing 60,000 people. The Great Fire of London broke out in 1666.

London became a center of the worldwide youth culture, partly as a result of the success of the Beatles and the Rolling Stones. London hosted the 2012 Olympics and Paralympics, making it the first city to host the modern games three times. Famous tourist destinations include Buckingham Palace, Westminster Abby, Tower of London, Big Ben, and the London Bridge.
